Hughski Ltd announces new OpenHardware ColorHugALS device

Thursday March 20th, 2015

London, UK — Hughski Limited announced this week that it is producing a new ColorHugALS device for ambient light backlight brightness control. An ambient light sensor is a chip that you’ve certainly got in your tablet, most probably in your phone, but very few laptops include one.

Ambient light sensors let us change the panel brightness automatically so that people can still see the screen when it’s sunny outside, but reducing the brightness when the ambient room light is lower to save power.

Hughski Limited has announced this new product due to the continued success of the original ColorHug device. The new applications are compatible with the existing ColorHug1 device, and the ColorHugALS is compatible with the existing applications.

About Hughski Limited:

Hughski is a small UK private limited company founded in 2011 and owned by Richard and Ania Hughes. The ColorHug is designed and built in London, England.
